It encompasses a wide range of styles, genres, and traditions, reflecting the country's cultural diversity and regional variations. Indian music is characterized by its intricate melodies, rhythmic patterns, and spiritual significance.


Indian classical music is divided into two main traditions: Hindustani (North Indian) and Carnatic (South Indian).

The various instruments used in the following musical forms are:

  • Carnatic Music
    • Mrudanga
    • Violin
    • Flute
    • Veena
    • Ghatam
    • Khanjira

  • Hindustani Music
    • Sitar
    • Tabla
    • Harmonium
    • Tanpura
    • Sarod
    • Shehnai
